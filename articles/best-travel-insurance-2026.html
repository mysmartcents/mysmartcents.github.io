Best Travel Insurance for International Trips 2026

SmartCents Editorial Team  ·  7 min read  ·  Updated 2026

Traveling internationally without insurance is one of the biggest financial risks you can take. A single medical emergency abroad can cost tens of thousands of dollars. Trip cancellations, lost luggage, and flight delays add up fast. The right travel insurance policy costs a fraction of what a single claim would — and gives you peace of mind on every trip.

What Does Travel Insurance Cover?

A comprehensive travel insurance plan typically covers the following:

  • Emergency medical expenses — Hospital bills, doctor fees, ambulance costs abroad
  • Medical evacuation — Emergency transport back to your home country if needed
  • Trip cancellation — Reimbursement if you cancel due to illness, death in family, or covered reasons
  • Trip interruption — Costs if your trip is cut short unexpectedly
  • Lost or delayed baggage — Compensation for lost, stolen, or delayed luggage
  • Flight delay — Meals and accommodation if your flight is significantly delayed
  • Personal liability — Coverage if you accidentally injure someone or damage property

How Much Does Travel Insurance Cost?

Travel insurance typically costs 4–8% of your total trip cost. For a $3,000 trip, expect to pay $120–$240 for comprehensive coverage. Factors that affect the price include your age, destination, trip length, and the level of coverage you choose.

For short trips and budget travelers, basic plans start from just a few dollars per day — making it one of the most cost-effective financial decisions you can make before any international trip.

What to Look for in a Travel Insurance Policy

  • Medical coverage limit — Minimum $100,000 for international trips. Some destinations (USA, Canada, Switzerland) require higher limits.
  • Emergency evacuation coverage — Should be at least $250,000. Medical evacuation flights can cost $50,000–$200,000.
  • Pre-existing conditions — Check whether your policy covers pre-existing medical conditions and what the waiver requirements are.
  • Adventure activities — If you plan to ski, scuba dive, or hike, ensure your policy covers adventure sports.
  • Cancel for any reason (CFAR) — Upgrade option that lets you cancel for any reason and get 50–75% back.
  • 24/7 assistance — Essential for international travel when emergencies happen at any hour.

Do I Need Travel Insurance If I Have Health Insurance?

Yes — almost always. Most domestic health insurance plans provide little or no coverage outside your home country. Even if they do cover some emergency care, they typically do not cover medical evacuation, trip cancellation, or lost baggage. Travel insurance fills these critical gaps.

Tips to Save Money on Travel Insurance

  • Buy as soon as you book — some benefits (like CFAR) require purchase within 14 days of your initial trip deposit
  • Consider annual multi-trip plans if you travel more than twice per year
  • Compare multiple providers before buying
  • Only buy coverage for what you actually need — if you have no checked bags, skip baggage coverage
  • Check if your credit card already provides some travel coverage

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I buy travel insurance?

Buy as soon as you book your trip. Purchasing early gives you the widest range of coverage options and ensures you are covered if something happens before your departure date.

Does travel insurance cover COVID-19?

Most modern travel insurance plans now cover COVID-19 related medical expenses and some trip cancellations. Check the specific policy terms before purchasing.

Can I buy travel insurance after booking?

Yes — you can purchase travel insurance any time before your departure. However, buying early gives you access to more benefits including pre-departure cancellation coverage.

Is travel insurance worth it for short trips?

Yes. Even on short trips, a medical emergency abroad can be financially devastating. A policy costing $20–50 for a weekend trip provides significant peace of mind.
